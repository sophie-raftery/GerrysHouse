📋 MISSION BRIEF

You descend into the basement armed with three vinyl records, a pocket full of evidence, and a growing sense of dread. This is where it all ends. The basement door has been locked for weeks — but you finally have the key.

What you find inside is not what you expected. The basement is set up like a shrine — old photographs on the walls, a record player in the corner, and in a small tank in the centre of the room: Patrick. Alive. Safe. Confused.

Jarry isn't a villain. He's a lonely man who moved Patrick down here to protect him from Fluffy, the family dog, who had been acting aggressively near the fish tank. The "mystery" of Patrick's disappearance was an act of love — misunderstood, but well-intentioned.

Now it's your job to piece together the full story, confront Jarry with the evidence, and reunite Patrick with the family he belongs to. The investigation is over. Justice — and Patrick — can finally go home.

🎯 OBJECTIVES

  • Use the basement key to unlock the door
  • Descend into the basement without triggering the final alarm
  • Locate Patrick's tank in the basement
  • Play all three vinyl records on the record player
  • Confront Jarry with the full evidence collection
  • Safely reunite Patrick with the family
  • Ensure Fluffy and Patrick can coexist safely
  • Complete the investigation and achieve the true ending

💡 TIPS & TRICKS

  • Play the vinyl records in order: MJ → Billy Joel → Katy Perry
  • Approach Jarry slowly — sudden movement triggers the bad ending
  • Check every corner of the basement before confronting Jarry
  • The record player is in the far corner next to an old armchair
  • Patrick is fine — focus on the confrontation, not the rescue
  • There are two endings: watch your choices carefully

🎬 THE ENDING

CASE CLOSED

The family returns home to find Jarry in the basement, sitting quietly beside Patrick's tank, playing old records on a worn-out player. The look on his face says everything: guilt, love, and a desperate wish that someone had simply asked.

Patrick is returned to the living room. Fluffy is given his own space. Jarry finally tells the truth — and for the first time in weeks, the house feels like a home again.

"Some mysteries don't have villains. Just people making the best decisions they can."
